Monthly SEO system

The monthly operating rhythm

Week Work Output Why it matters
Week 1 Review Search Console, GBP performance, WhatsApp enquiries, and rankings. One monthly scorecard and 3 priority fixes. Do not chase random keywords. Let search data and enquiries decide the next work.
Week 2 Publish one high-intent financing page or article. Example: "Business loan rejection in Malaysia: what to prepare before reapplying". Google recommends useful, unique, people-first content, not thin keyword pages.
Week 3 Update Google Business Profile and request reviews from suitable clients. 4 GBP posts, fresh photos, review replies, and complete business info. Local visibility is driven by relevance, distance, and prominence.
Week 4 Repurpose the monthly topic into social and short-form assets. 1 LinkedIn post, 1 Instagram carousel, 1 short video script, 1 WhatsApp follow-up angle. SEO compounds slowly; social and partner traffic create faster discovery.

Traffic channels

Best traffic mix for EGA

1. Google Search

Build service pages around intent, not volume. Start with SME business loan consultant, working capital financing, equipment financing, grant financing, factoring, P2P financing, halal financing, and business loan rejection support.

2. Google Business Profile

This should become Jay's local trust surface: complete info, recent photos, weekly posts, review replies, service categories, and clear appointment/WhatsApp path.

3. LinkedIn and founder-led posts

Post practical financing breakdowns for founders, accountants, SME owners, and business consultants. Do not write generic "we help businesses grow" content.

4. Referral partners

Accountants, company secretaries, insurance agents, real estate agents, SME consultants, and equipment suppliers can send higher-intent leads than broad social posting.

5. Low-budget search ads

Use only for high-intent terms once conversion tracking is installed. Send traffic to one tight page with WhatsApp click tracking, not the homepage by default.

6. Case-story assets

Publish anonymized before/after stories: funding problem, documents missing, route chosen, time to approval, and lesson learned. No fake claims, no guaranteed approval language.

GPT Image content engine

Weekly content automation engine

Goal: one financing lesson becomes five assets every week.

The engine should not mass-produce generic financial quotes. It should turn Jay's actual advisory knowledge into clear, trust-building content.

Inputs

  • One real client question or anonymized financing situation.
  • One financing route: bank, grant, factoring, P2P, halal, koperasi, or equity.
  • One caution: documents, cash flow, CTOS/CCRIS, collateral, approval risk, or timeline.

  • 5-slide Instagram carousel using GPT Image in EGA navy/gold/cream.

Important guardrails

Claim safety for finance content

Use

  • "Funding route check" instead of "guaranteed approval".
  • "Subject to lender approval" on calculators and financing examples.
  • Real client outcomes only, anonymized when needed.
  • Clear disclaimers when content is educational.

Avoid

  • Guaranteed approval, guaranteed rates, or guaranteed timelines.
  • Fake screenshots, fake bank letters, or invented case studies.
  • Thin AI-generated articles that repeat the same advice.
  • Mass content across unrelated finance topics.
